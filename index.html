Frequently Asked Questions

  • Drowsiness refers to a state of being sleepy or lethargic, often characterized by reduced alertness and a strong urge to sleep. It can result from various factors such as inadequate sleep, certain medications, medical conditions like sleep disorders, or even boredom. Drowsiness can affect cognitive functions, reaction times, and overall performance, making it dangerous when involved in activities that require attention and concentration, such as driving or operating machinery.

  • We have designed a device called SafeDrive to prevent drivers from falling asleep while driving on highways and enhance the road safety. These smart anti-sleeping glasses incorporate IOT and Technology to detect the signs of driver fatigue and intervene before accidents occur.

  • Equipped with special sensors, these glasses can detect when a driver is feeling sleepy. Once sleepiness is detected, the glasses activate an alert system, which includes a loud buzzer to wake up the driver and a message sent to the vehicle's onboard computer to slow down. If the driver doesn't wake up after several alerts, the glasses instruct the vehicle to stop completely, preventing potential accidents. Additionally, the glasses turn on special parking lights to alert other drivers of the stopped vehicle.

  • The glasses send a message to the manager of the truck or bus, providing the live location of the vehicle and alerting them to the driver's condition, enabling timely intervention and assistance. This integrated approach ensures that drivers remain alert and safe while on the road, while also notifying relevant parties in case of emergencies.
